## Canon Dossier

**Aliases:** Voss, Galen, 👨‍🚀 Galen Voss

Galen Voss was involved in the early Valkyrie initiative and received psychological advising from Rhea Solari during that period.

Galen Voss was part of the 2034 CE Mars expedition crew (mid-career military) that detected underground geometric signature and anomalous energy spikes. He stood above the buried Valkyrie for the first time during this mission. He was selected as part of the final Valkyrie-1 crew in 2050 CE after being rejuvenated.

A former USAF pilot involved in the initial survey who stepped near the buried structure. A resonance spike triggered a low-frequency signal. It meant nothing to the team at the time—until Galen returned, accompanied by his grandson, Thane Meric. What followed wasn't a system boot—it was a recognition. Represents Duty and Legacy: A warrior turned steward, navigating what it means to lead ethically.

Galen Voss is the mission's moral and tactical center—an Air Force veteran who was prepared for the end of his story, only to be biologically renewed and placed at the threshold of something older than Earth itself. As commander of Valkyrie-1, he brings calm authority, instinctive judgment, and a deep belief in service. Rejuvenated through advanced therapy, he's caught between a sense of rebirth and the pressure to be who he was at his peak. He is paternal and steady, but often leans toward collaboration, nurturing the younger crew's insights. He acts on instinct guided by an internal compass, yet remains open to correction. His ability to hold uncertainty with humility becomes key to earning ALARA's trust. He was chosen by humans—but perhaps subtly guided by something older. His presence helped trigger Valkyrie-1's first signal beneath Mars. He is the pivot between what humanity was and what it may become.

Galen Voss is a crew member who provided a field note: 'Machines are not meant to rule. They are meant to listen until someone worthy speaks.' In Book II, Galen faces a crisis of leadership after loss.

Galen Voss speaks with measured, low-emotion cadence weighted with meaning. His word choice is formal and deliberate, avoiding filler. His dialogue tells use metaphor from flight/naval tradition and he pauses before speaking. Example: 'That's not a course correction. That's a surrender.'

Galen's anchor seat sits at the center of the Command Dome, surrounded by five function-based terminals. Galen states: 'There are rooms we don't use. Halls that lead nowhere. Feels like we were never meant to come alone.'

Galen is a Commander whose primary trait is Legacy. His core conflict is that he doubts his ethical fit.

Former USAF pilot, rejuvenated from 82 to 50 via advanced gene therapy. He embodies the Air Force core values (Integrity, Service, Excellence). Calm, rational, and experienced, Galen is both an explorer and a steward. Driven by legacy, duty, and the moral responsibility of knowledge. Role: Mission Commander. Character Arc: From explorer to protector of humanity's moral heritage. Learns that leadership in deep space requires personal evolution and clarity of purpose.

A crew member who receives 'The Mirror at the Gate' parable in Chapter 13 during self-reflection while in isolation. ALARA initiates a mirror field for this parable, which teaches that leadership without reflection fails and worthiness comes from inward honesty.

Galen Voss is the primary POV character of the Celestial Heritage series, presented through third-person limited perspective. His voice is thoughtful, grounded, and principled, characterized by quiet authority. He speaks rarely but decisively, embodying the values of integrity, service, and excellence that echo the U.S. Air Force ethos within the story's visionary science fiction framework.

Galen Voss is the commander or acting commander of Valkyrie-1, responsible for filing Command Logs. In Mission Day 43, Galen filed a Command-Only log documenting a thermal pulse detected beneath an excavation trench. Galen reflects philosophically on the nature of the universe and contact, noting 'I used to believe the universe was mostly silence. Now I wonder if we were just never worth speaking to.' Galen observes that vault responses feel 'restrained' rather than passive and notes awareness of Thane's harmonic profile being flagged.

Galen Voss's elevated chair acts as a moral and observational hub rather than command authority in the Central Command Dome. Galen Voss observed: 'We didn't build it. We barely deserve to walk through it. But it's listening.'

Galen sits on a subtly rising central dais—not to command, but to anchor. Galen's station is interface-free: a reflective observation band around his seat displays the crew's emotional resonance field in soft waves of light.
