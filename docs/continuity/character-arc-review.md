# Character Arc Review Rubric

**Scope:** `content/wiki/arcs/characters/*.md`

**Purpose:** Ensure character arc ledgers describe what is in the book, guide
review questions, and provide safe foundations for future/speculative ASK
answers without turning speculation into canon.

## Review Inputs

Before reviewing an arc ledger, gather:

- The character page in `content/wiki/characters/`.
- Relevant chapter pages in `content/wiki/stories/`.
- Relevant mission-log summaries in `content/wiki/mission-logs/index.md`.
- Continuity notes that affect chronology or identity, especially
  `docs/continuity/mission-log-audit.md`.

## Pass / Fix / Block

- **Pass:** The ledger can be used by ASK and future reviewers.
- **Fix:** The ledger is usable after local edits; no author decision needed.
- **Block:** The ledger requires an author decision, unresolved source conflict,
  or missing chapter evidence before approval.

## Evidence Questions

- Does every material claim cite chapter text, mission logs, or reviewed wiki
  canon?
- Are generated dossier sections used only as support, not as scene proof?
- If chapter text and derived summaries disagree, does chapter text win?
- Are chronology-sensitive claims checked against mission day/date caveats?
- Are absent or off-page beats labeled as absent rather than invented?

## Character Continuity Questions

- Is each chapter entry a real state change, pressure, or consequence?
- Does the ledger separate what happened from how the character changed?
- Does each "state after" follow from the prior state?
- Are major choices distinct from passive exposure to events?
- Does the arc preserve the character's established voice, limits, and role?

## Interpretation Discipline Questions

- Are interpretive claims phrased as readings rather than hidden facts?
- Is the evidence strong enough for the stated confidence level?
- Are alternative readings noted when ambiguity appears intentional?
- Does the ledger avoid moralizing beyond what the text supports?
- Are theme-level conclusions grounded in specific scenes?

## Speculative Readiness Questions

- Are future-facing questions framed as unknowns?
- Do speculation guardrails say what ASK must not infer?
- Does the ledger avoid answering Book II+ questions from Book I evidence?
- Can ASK use the ledger to say "we do not know yet" with useful context?
- Are unresolved tensions distinct from contradictions?

## Spoiler Safety Questions

- Can each claim be gated to a chapter boundary?
- Are end-state claims withheld until the relevant chapter?
- Does the ledger avoid summarizing the full arc in a way that leaks late-book
  revelations to early readers?
- Are mission-log-only spoilers treated with the same chapter boundary as the
  log's chapter?
